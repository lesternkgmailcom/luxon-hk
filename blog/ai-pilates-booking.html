My AI booked a Pilates class for me — because it knows me.

I had a gap after work on Tuesday. I told my AI, and it booked my favourite class with my preferred instructor — because it remembers my preferences from past bookings.

April 2026 · 2 min read

Tuesday afternoon. I finish work and realize I have a gap before dinner. I want to work out, but I don't want to spend 10 minutes scrolling through the Mindbody app checking schedules.

So I send my AI a message:

"I have a gap after work tonight. Book a Pilates class for me."

30 seconds later:

"Booked ✅ Reformer Pilates with Sabrina at Empower Fitness, 7:15 PM. Added to your calendar. Same studio as last week."

Why this is different from Siri or Google Assistant

Siri can set a reminder. Google Assistant can search the web. But my AI:

  • Knows my schedule — it has access to my calendar, so it knows what "after work" means today specifically
  • Remembers my preferences — Reformer Pilates, not mat. Sabrina as instructor. Empower Fitness in Central
  • Learns from past bookings — it's booked classes for me before, so it knows the drill
  • Takes action, not just suggests — it actually books the class and adds it to my calendar, not just tells me where to go

The difference is context. My AI has been with me long enough to build up a picture of what I like, when I'm free, and how I prefer things done.

The memory advantage

Every interaction makes it better. Over time, my AI has learned:

Studio preference: Empower Fitness, 19/F Li Dong Building, Central

Class type: Reformer Pilates

Preferred instructor: Sabrina Lui

Preferred time: Weekday evenings, 7:00-8:00 PM

Booking lead time: Same day is fine, but popular classes fill up by afternoon

I never had to write any of this down in a settings panel. The AI picked it up from watching my patterns and confirming with me when unsure.

What else it handles

Class booking is just one example. The same memory and action-taking applies to:

  • Restaurant reservations — "Book dinner for 2 Friday night, somewhere in Soho, around HK$400/head"
  • Flight bookings — "Find me a flight to Taipei next weekend, direct if possible"
  • Doctor appointments — "Book a check-up with Dr. Chan next week, morning preferred"
  • Meeting scheduling — "Move my Thursday 3pm to Friday morning, the client requested it"

The pattern is always the same: I describe what I need in plain language, and my AI handles the rest — because it knows me.

The point

An AI assistant isn't a chatbot that answers questions. It's something that acts on your behalf — with enough context about your life to make good decisions without asking you 20 follow-up questions.

That's what we set up for people. Not a tool you have to learn — an assistant that learns you.
