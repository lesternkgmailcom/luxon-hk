My AI knows my resting heart rate. It told me to go all-out.

The same AI that reads my emails, signs my contracts, and books my Pilates classes also acts as my personal trainer — analyzing training load, HRV, and recovery data.

April 2026 · 2 min read

My AI knows my resting heart rate, my HRV, my sleep score, and my training load history. It told me this morning:

"You're NOT detrained. CTL of 29 is solid — not your peak of 37, but not a rebuild situation either."

Then it checked my recovery metrics — resting HR of 49, HRV of 53, sleep score of 94 — and said:

"Your body is in great shape for intervals. Go all-out tomorrow. Just don't stack multiple high-load days without a recovery day."

This isn't a fitness app

This is my AI assistant — the same one that reads my emails, signs my contracts, and books my Pilates classes. It just happens to know my training data too.

How? Because it has access to my health data. It pulls my training metrics, understands the context (what I've been training for, when my next event is), and gives me personalized advice — not generic "get more sleep" tips.

One AI, every part of my life

The point isn't that AI can be a fitness coach. The point is that one assistant handles everything:

  • Work — reads my emails, drafts replies, signs contracts, manages my calendar
  • Life — books Pilates, makes reservations, tracks flights
  • Health — analyzes my training load, recovery metrics, and recommends workouts
  • Finance — sends me daily market briefings, stock price alerts, commodity tracking

No app switching. No data silos. No separate logins for 10 different tools.

The difference between 10 apps and 1 assistant

I used to have a training app, an email app, a calendar app, a note-taking app, a finance app, a booking app... Each one isolated. None of them connected.

Now I have one AI that connects them all. When my AI tells me to go all-out tomorrow, it already knows I don't have early meetings. When it books my Pilates class, it already knows my training load that day.

That context is what makes it different. Not smarter algorithms — just more context about your life.

How it works

The setup is simple:

1. Connect your data — give your AI access to the apps and data sources you already use (email, calendar, health, fitness)

2. Tell it what you need — in plain language, describe your goals, preferences, and routines

3. It learns and adapts — every interaction makes it better. It remembers your patterns and builds context over time

No coding. No complex setup. Just a conversation.

The point

An AI assistant isn't a fitness tracker, an email client, or a calendar. It's one thing that understands all of them together — and uses that understanding to actually help you, across every part of your life.

That's what we set up for people. Not a tool you have to learn — an assistant that learns you.
