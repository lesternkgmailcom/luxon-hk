My AI briefs me on gold, oil, and markets every morning.

Every morning, my AI sends me a market briefing: gold, oil, S&P 500, Hang Seng, Nikkei — with daily change percentages, context on what moved the markets, and what it means for my portfolio. No financial app needed.

April 2026 · 2 min read

Working in transpacific trade, I need to stay on top of global markets — not just for trading, but because commodity prices directly affect shipping costs, supplier pricing, and contract negotiations.

The old way: open Bloomberg, check gold, check oil, check US indices, check Asian indices. Scroll through headlines. Piece together the story yourself. Maybe 15-20 minutes every morning.

Now I get one message. Here's what it looked like on April 9, 2026:

Daily Market Brief — 9 April 2026, 12:31 PM HKT

Gold (XAU/USD): ~$4,835/oz | Daily: +2.7% | MTD: +3.5% | QTD: -4.4% | YTD: ~+1%

Gold hit an all-time high of $5,061 on Feb 11; currently retracing amid geopolitical de-escalation.


Brent Oil: ~$93.90/bbl | Daily: -14% | MTD: -12% | QTD: +18% | YTD: +25%

Crashed after US-Iran 2-week ceasefire deal and Strait of Hormuz reopening. Steepest oil drop in ~6 years.


Key Indices:

S&P 500: 6,616.85 (+0.08%, futures +2.7%)

Dow Jones: 46,584.46 (-0.18%, futures +2.5%)

Nasdaq: 22,017.85 (+0.10%, futures +3.3%)

Hang Seng: ~22,900 (+2.95%)

Nikkei 225: 56,308.42 (+5.39%)

One message. Gold, oil, five indices. Plus the context — why oil crashed (US-Iran ceasefire), where gold is relative to its high, what futures are signaling for the next session.

Not just numbers — context

A raw price feed is useless without context. My AI doesn't just dump numbers — it adds the story behind the moves:

  • Geopolitical drivers — “Oil crashed after US-Iran ceasefire deal” — tells me this isn't a normal market move
  • Historical context — “Steepest oil drop in ~6 years” — tells me the magnitude
  • Futures vs cash — S&P closed flat but futures are +2.7% — tells me what to expect at the next open
  • Multi-timeframe view — daily, MTD, QTD, YTD — tells me if this is a blip or a trend

Fully customizable

The briefing is configured to my needs. I can:

  • Add or remove assets — add VIX, USD/JPY, copper, or any commodity I'm tracking
  • Change delivery time — 7 AM before I wake up, or 9 AM just before market open
  • Add alerts — “flag if gold breaks $5,000 or oil drops below $90”
  • Change format — brief summary or detailed breakdown

Who is this for?

  • Procurement and trade professionals — commodity prices affect your costs directly
  • Investors with day jobs — stay informed without living in a finance app
  • Business owners — understand how macro moves affect your business planning

You don't need a Bloomberg terminal. You need an AI that reads one and tells you what matters — in one message, every morning.
