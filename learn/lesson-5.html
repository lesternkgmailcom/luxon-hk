Say What TO Do

Tell the AI what you want — not what you don't want. One rule that changes everything.

"Don't" tells AI what's wrong. "Do" tells it what's right. Same idea — completely different results.

1

The pattern

Negative instructions ("don't do X") leave the AI guessing. It knows what to avoid, but not what to aim for. That ambiguity leads to inconsistent, sometimes bizarre results.

2

The fix

Flip every "don't" into a positive instruction. "Don't use jargon" → "Use plain language." "Don't make it long" → "Keep it under 300 words." Simple rewrite, massive improvement.

3

Be specific about format

Don't just say what you want — say how you want it. Bullet points? One page? A table? A table with 3 columns? The more specific the format, the less revision you'll need.

Before

"Don't make it too long, don't use big words, don't add fluff"

After

"Keep it under 300 words, use simple language, focus on key takeaways"

💡
Universal rule: Both OpenAI and Anthropic agree on this — positive instructions dramatically outperform negative ones. It's the single most impactful prompting change you can make.
