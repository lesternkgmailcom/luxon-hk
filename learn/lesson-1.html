AI vs Google Search

Why giving AI more context gets you dramatically better results.

Same question — two different approaches. The longer one gets dramatically better answers.

1

The problem: thinking like Google

Most people type short keywords into AI, like they're searching Google. "EV comparison" or "write email." AI is not a search engine — it's a conversation partner. The more context you give, the better it understands what you actually need.

2

The fix: use the Task + Context formula

Every good AI prompt has two parts: what you want done and what the AI needs to know. Think of it like briefing a new employee — they can't read your mind, but if you tell them your situation, they'll nail it.

3

Include your constraints

Budget, timeline, audience, format — these filters turn a generic answer into a useful one. "Summarize this" vs "Summarize this in 3 bullet points for a non-technical manager" produces completely different (and better) results.

💡
Try this now: Open your AI and ask it the same question you normally would. Then re-ask with 3x more context. Compare the results. The difference is usually shocking.
