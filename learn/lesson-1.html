AI vs Google Search

Why giving AI more context gets you dramatically better results.

L
You 10:02 AM
EV comparison
Lx
Lx
AI 10:02 AM
Sure! Here are some popular electric vehicles on the market:

🚗 Tesla Model 3 — from ~HK$280k
🚗 BYD Atto 3 — from ~HK$190k
🚗 BMW iX3 — from ~HK$380k
🚗 Hyundai Ioniq 5 — from ~HK$300k

Would you like me to compare specific models?

That's how most people talk to AI. Short keywords. Like a Google search. And the AI gives you… a Google search result. Generic. Not wrong — just not useful.

Try again
L
You 10:05 AM
I'm comparing EVs for a family of 4 in Hong Kong. Budget HK$300k. Need boot space for 2 strollers. Prefer white. We do mostly city driving, occasional trips to Shenzhen. What are my best options?
Lx
Lx
AI 10:05 AM
Based on your situation, here's what I'd narrow it to:

🥇 BYD Seal — HK$259k, 540L boot (fits 2 strollers), available in white, great for city + cross-border charging network

🥈 Tesla Model 3 Highland — HK$265k, 682L boot, Supercharger network across HK + Shenzhen, white available

🥉 Hyundai Ioniq 5 — HK$299k, 531L boot, V2L can power appliances during Shenzhen trips

Want me to compare insurance costs or depreciation for these three?

Same question. One is 3 words. The other is 3 sentences. The second answer is 10x more useful. The secret? Task + Context.

Same mistake at work
L
You 2:30 PM
Write an email about the delayed shipment
Lx
Lx
AI 2:30 PM
I'd be happy to help! Could you tell me:

• Who is this going to?
• What was delayed and why?
• What's the new timeline?
• What tone do you want — formal, friendly, urgent?

The AI is asking you the questions it needs. But you already know the answers. Why not give them all at once?

Here's how it should look
L
You 2:35 PM
Write an email to my supplier in Shenzhen about the delayed shipment. Container was supposed to arrive last Friday, now they say next Wednesday. Our warehouse is in Tuen Mun. Tone: firm but professional. Ask for an explanation and updated ETA. CC our logistics manager David.
Lx
Lx
AI 2:35 PM
Done. Here's the draft:

---

Dear [Supplier Name],

We note that container [number], originally scheduled for arrival on April 11th, has been rescheduled to April 16th without prior notification.

As this delay impacts our delivery commitments, we would appreciate an explanation for the revised timeline and confirmation that the new ETA of April 16th is firm.

Our warehouse is located in Tuen Mun — please ensure the delivery booking reflects this.

We look forward to your prompt response.

Best regards
[Your Name]

---

Want me to adjust the tone or add anything?

Every good prompt has two parts: what you want done and what the AI needs to know. Budget, timeline, audience, tone, format — these filters turn a generic answer into a useful one.

Constraints change everything
